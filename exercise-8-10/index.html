Project Overview

Company: Mercer Paints - Paint manufacturing company located in Alabama

Network Setup: Uses a network to link its business operations with potential security vulnerabilities identified

Objective: Conduct comprehensive security risk assessment, identify threats, assign probabilities, estimate annual losses, and provide strategic recommendations for risk mitigation

Tools Used: Spreadsheet software for calculations, risk assessment data analysis, and loss estimation modeling

Additional Potential Threats Identified

In addition to the primary threats analyzed, our assessment identified three additional potential threats to Mercer Paints' network infrastructure:

1. Social Engineering Attacks

Probability: 12% annually | Potential Loss: $4,500 | Annual Expected Loss: $540

Description: Employees may be targeted through phishing emails, phone calls, or physical manipulation to gain unauthorized access to company systems or reveal sensitive information. This is particularly concerning for a manufacturing company where employees may not have extensive cybersecurity training.

2. Insider Threats (Disgruntled Employees)

Probability: 6% annually | Potential Loss: $8,000 | Annual Expected Loss: $480

Description: Current or former employees with legitimate access could intentionally sabotage systems, steal proprietary paint formulas, or leak customer information. Manufacturing environments often have trusted employee access to critical systems.

3. Supply Chain Cyber Attacks

Probability: 4% annually | Potential Loss: $12,000 | Annual Expected Loss: $480

Description: Cyber attacks targeting Mercer Paints through compromised supplier systems or software updates. Paint manufacturers rely on chemical suppliers and logistics partners whose security breaches could impact operations.

Additional Threats Total Annual Loss: $1,500 ($540 + $480 + $480)

Combined Total Expected Annual Loss: $3,500 (Original $2,000 + Additional $1,500)

Priority Recommendations for Mercer Paints

  1. Inventory and Asset Management (Already Done): Complete comprehensive asset inventory and implement discovery tools to maintain visibility of all network devices and potential entry points.
  2. Vulnerability Assessment (Already Done): Conduct thorough vulnerability scanning and assessment to identify all potential security weaknesses in current infrastructure.
  3. Create Priority List (Already Done): Establish risk-based priority system focusing on highest impact threats first, particularly hardware/system failures.
  4. Fix Hardware / Software Issues: Address critical hardware vulnerabilities immediately, implement backup systems, and establish regular maintenance protocols to prevent $900 annual loss from system failures.
  5. Staff Training: Implement comprehensive security awareness training program to reduce human error factors contributing to malware, social engineering, and unauthorized access incidents. Target the $940 annual loss from social engineering and insider threats.
  6. Monitor: Deploy continuous monitoring solutions including SIEM systems, network traffic analysis, and endpoint detection to provide real-time threat visibility and detect insider threats.
  7. Audit: Establish regular third-party security audits and internal compliance reviews to ensure ongoing security posture improvement and supply chain security validation.

Final Recommendation

Immediate Action Required: Focus on hardware/system reliability improvements as this represents the highest single risk ($900 of $3,500 total annual loss). Implement redundant systems and comprehensive backup solutions within 90 days.

Human Factor Focus: Address social engineering and insider threats through comprehensive training programs, as these represent $1,020 combined annual risk and are often the easiest entry points for attackers.

Long-term Strategy: Develop integrated security program combining in-house capabilities with external expertise for specialized needs. This hybrid approach provides cost-effective security while maintaining rapid response capabilities.

Expected ROI: Investment in comprehensive security program should reduce annual risk exposure by 60-80%, potentially saving $2,100-$2,800 annually while significantly improving operational resilience.
